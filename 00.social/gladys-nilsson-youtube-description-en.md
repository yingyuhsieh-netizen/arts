Step into Gladys Nilsson’s exuberant world, where watercolor refuses to whisper and not a single inch of paper is allowed to sit quietly. Her stretched bodies, saturated colors, and tightly packed scenes turn ordinary life into a gleefully unruly theater.

From her working-class childhood on Chicago’s North Side to the rebellious Hairy Who, this film follows an artist who made “celebrating small things” a lifelong practice. Discover how pregnancy led Nilsson from oils to watercolor in 1962, how precise pencil structures and transparent washes gave that medium unexpected weight, and how works including *Kold Toddiebears* (1970), *Dipped Dick: Adam and Eve After Cranach* (1971), *Beset by Small Problems* (1990), and *Rounding Rosie’s Ring: Dance, You Fools, Dance* (2024) transformed awkward gestures into visual comedy.

The story also traces Nilsson’s insistence on making women larger, more commanding, and impossible to contain—an impulse that gained particular force after her landmark 1973 solo exhibition at the Whitney Museum of American Art.
